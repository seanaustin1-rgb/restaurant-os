# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small-business **operators** across six sectors — restaurant/hospitality, service
businesses, contractors/field service, real-estate brokerages, vacation-rental
managers, and retail. The core user is a time-poor owner checking in (often on a
phone, often after hours) who needs to know one thing fast: *am I safe, am I
profitable, and what's pressuring me right now?* They are financially responsible
but not accountants, and they will not sit through setup before seeing value.

Secondary roles share a business but with strict ceilings (see the access model):
**consultants/accountants** who prepare setup, **investors** who view a read-only
heartbeat after approval, and **managers** who run daily ops. The owner owns the
data; everyone else acts only after the owner approves.

## Product Purpose

OutFront Data turns scattered financial signals — bank (Plaid), POS (Toast),
and review platforms — into an **industry-specific heartbeat**: a Profit First
allocation layer, leak-detection tiles, a reputation/"Aura" read, sector
benchmarks, and a public no-login prospect demo. Each sector is genuinely
modeled (its own accounts, targets, scale anchor, and signature metric), not one
restaurant dashboard relabeled.

Success is the owner glancing once and *knowing* — informed, never judged, never
trapped in setup, never sold an answer the numbers don't support. The heartbeat
shows before every source is connected (manual estimates → sample tours → live
data all lead to action).

## Brand Personality

Clear, grounded, financially disciplined — with a restrained cool factor. The
voice of a sharp operator-friendly CFO: plain-spoken, calm, credible the way a
good ledger is credible. It teaches while it diagnoses (shows the math, explains
the money in plain language) and warms through copper + serif + real data, never
through hype. Three words: **clear, disciplined, warm-but-exact.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** — cream-and-navy palette, the gradient hero-metric template,
  the endless identical icon + heading + text card grid.
- **The bank-connection scare screen** — fear-driven "link your account now" flows.
- **Over-technical accounting software** — meaning buried in jargon and ledgers.
- **The flashy AI toy** — glassmorphism, decorative glows/shadows, gradient text;
  promising intelligence without showing real numbers.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the heartbeat first.** Deliver meaningful financial feedback before
   every source is connected; never trap the owner in setup before value.
2. **Trust ahead of automation.** Show the math, never overclaim. Every status
   carries a label, number, or icon — never color alone — and every read is
   honest about what drove it.
3. **Plain language over jargon.** Explain the money like a good mentor would;
   teach while diagnosing. No accountant-speak the owner has to decode.
4. **Each industry feels modeled, not relabeled.** A contractor's experience
   differs from a brokerage's or a restaurant's in metrics, copy, and emphasis —
   not just the words on the same template.
5. **Restraint as the default.** Ration color and ornament; the "cool factor"
   comes from precision, exact figures, and purposeful motion, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Financial status must never rely on color alone — pair
every green/yellow/red signal with a label, number, or icon, and keep it legible
to a color-blind user with color removed. Verify AA contrast on the dark surface
(body text ≥4.5:1; large/bold ≥3:1; placeholders held to 4.5:1, not muted gray).
Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade/instant alternative for every
animation. Keep tap targets ≥44px; design mobile-first (the owner's quick check
happens on a phone).
